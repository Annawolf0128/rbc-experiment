You are participating in a group game with the other {{ other_players }} players. The game lasts {{ num_rounds }} rounds, and the rules are the same in every round.

Each round, every participant chooses an integer between 0 and 100.

After everyone submits their numbers, the group median is computed.

The group median is the middle value among the {{ n }} numbers chosen.

For example, if the numbers chosen are 10, 30, 45, 60, and 80, the group median is 45.

If your number equals the median or is above it, you pay no penalty.

If your number is strictly below the median, you pay the penalty of {{ L }}. This is a fixed penalty: being slightly below the median and being far below the median both lead to the same penalty.

Your earnings each round

Your earnings = {{ E }} − Cost − Penalty (if below the median), where:

  • Cost = (your number)² ÷ {{ K }}. Higher numbers cost more. On the choice page, the cost of your selected number will be calculated and shown to you. Your cost only depends on the number of your choice, not by numbers chosen by others.
  • Penalty of {{ L }} applies only if your number is strictly below the group median that round.
Feedback after each round

After each round, you will see the group median and your earnings for that round. You will not see other participants' choices.

Payment

At the end of the experiment, one round will be selected at random and you will receive your earnings from that round, plus a fixed show-up fee of $5. The exchange rate between points and dollars is 2:1: for example, 1 point is worth $0.50.
